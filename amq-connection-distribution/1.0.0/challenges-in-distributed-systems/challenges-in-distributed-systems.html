Challenges in Distributed Systems

Distributed systems face several challenges due to their inherent complexity:

  • Direct Communication Issues: Synchronous, direct service-to-service calls can lead to tight coupling, bottlenecks, and cascading failures.

  • Asynchronous Processing Need: Many operations don’t require immediate responses, making synchronous communication inefficient and resource-intensive.

  • Reliability and Data Consistency: Ensuring messages are delivered, processed exactly once, and data remains consistent across multiple services is difficult.

  • Scalability and Coupling: Tightly coupled services hinder independent scaling and make systems brittle when changes occur.
